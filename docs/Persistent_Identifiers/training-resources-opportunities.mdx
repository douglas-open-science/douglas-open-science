# Training resources and opportunities
The McGill Library frequently offers training on Open Science practices, including on the use of PIDs such as ORCID. Please consult [their calendar](https://libraryrooms.mcgill.ca/calendar/mcgill) for the next scheduled events.

Visit the [PID webpage](https://www.crkn-rcdr.ca/en/persistent-identifiers) of the Canadian Research Knowledge Network (CRKN), which is leading the development of a national PID strategy, to learn about current initiatives surrounding the use of PIDs in Canada and for support with infrastructural initiatives. The CRKN has additionally recorded a webinar series about PIDs in partnership with CARL-Portage and Research Data Canada: “[The Who, What, and Where of Persistent Identifiers: A six-part webinar series devoted to all things PID](https://www.crkn-rcdr.ca/en/who-what-and-where-persistent-identifiers-six-part-webinar-series-devoted-all-things-pid)“.

For an introduction to PIDs and particularly to the Canadian context, we recommend “[Persistent Identifiers as Open Research Infrastructure to Reduce Administrative Burden](https://popjournal.ca/issue03/goddard)”, by Lisa Goddard, University of Victoria.

#### McGill University
The McGill Library provides assistance with [creating and using ORCID accounts](https://libraryguides.mcgill.ca/ORCID) and with [using the McGill University Dataverse repository](https://www.mcgill.ca/libraries/research-services/data-services/sharing/dataverse), which assigns DOIs to hosted datasets.
